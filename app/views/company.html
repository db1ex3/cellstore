Investors & Advisors

Roger Bamford

Roger Bamford is one of the founding members of Oracle's database team and has been with Oracle since 1984. He was the lead for Oracle's relational database management system version 6 and is known as the founding father of Oracle Real Application Clusters and Oracle's grid products. Bamford currently heads the Advanced Research and Development team focused on distributed and parallel computing. In his current role, Bamford provides overall architectural leadership to the flagship database product, including XML, XQuery, and event processing technologies. Throughout his career, Bamford has been responsible for many of the key innovations in relational database management systems. He has more than 40 patents on databases, and has presented and has been published in numerous international conferences. Bamford holds a BS in computer science from Massachusetts Institute of Technology and an MS in electrical engineering from Stanford University.

Prof. Donald Kossmann

Donald Kossmann is a professor for Computer Science at ETH Zurich (Switzerland). He received his MS in 1991 from the University of Karlsruhe and completed his PhD in 1995 at the Technical University of Aachen. After that, he held positions at the University of Maryland, the IBM Almaden Research Center, the University of Passau, the Technical University of Munich, and the University of Heidelberg. He is a former associate editor of ACM Transactions on Databases and ACM Transactions on Internet Technology. He is an ACM fellow, a member of the board of trustees of the VLDB endowment, and was the program committee chair of the ACM SIGMOD Conf., 2009. He is a co-founder of i-TV-T (1998), XQRL Inc. (acquired by BEA in 2002), and 28msec Inc. (2007). His research interests lie in the area of databases and information systems.

Dr. Daniela Florescu

Dr. Florescu is an expert in query languages and query processing, focusing on semi-structured data. She spent 20 years in various roles, researcher (ATT Research, IBM, INRIA- the French National Research in Computer Science), investor and entrepreneur (XQRL, 28msec), professor and lecturer (University of Paris VI, Stanford), as well as contributor to small and large companies (Oracle, BEA). She is the author of XQuery, the standard XML Query language, and JSONiq- the JSON query language. She is currently a software architect at Oracle, managing the Zorba open source team and delivering the world's first semi-structured data processing platform.
